Perceived Percent: This is the percent that a woman will perceive a man to be. Throughout a woman's life the importance of physical traits (Height or Leanness) vs provisioning traits (Income) shift. When a woman considers a man, the perceived percent is that value that she internalizes.

Actual Percent: This is the unmitigated percent that a man is at. It is more of a true value than the obfuscated value that perception introduces.

Practical Difference: A practical way of understanding this difference would be to imagine an average man (5' 9" and 198 Pounds) in his 20's that makes above average income ($105K) and is seeking a woman in her 20's. In her 20's she will prioritize looks (Height/BMI) over income. So, she will see him a man at the 75th percentile (SMV 1.25) and probably not take any interest. Strictly speaking though, his income is in the top 13% of men thus putting him at the 23rd percentile.

Ultimately, only perceived SMV matters, but context is helpful. The man in the prior example needs to lose weight. Earning more money will help, but not as much as dropping 40 pounds.
