Description

This simulation determines the results of a woman attempting to have children over a specific interval. This simulation assumes that the woman will attempt to get pregnant consistently and without a break. Understandably, this is not completely reasonable. This assumption means that a woman will attempt to get pregnant right after every miscarriage.

This is a statistical analysis exercised through computer simulation. Set the characteristics for a given scenario and click on the "Submit" button.

This simulation will generate 1,000 women then simulate them attempting to have children month after month. The constraint of 1,000 women was set to improve response performance. With higher number, you get greater accuracy. If you want a simulation with 1 Million women, then contact me for a quote. For the general inquiry, 1,000 women is sufficient. The number are generally accurate to within 0.1 children.
